Welcome!

E Komo Mai! Welcome to the Hawaiian Monk Seal Directory, where you can search our seal database, learn more about Hawaii’s state mammal, and explore how to responsibly share the ocean and beaches with these amazing animals. Built in partnership with NOAA Fisheries and The Marine Mammal Center – Ke Kai Ola, we aim to raise awareness for this endangered species (~1,400 individuals remaining) and highlight practical ways that local community members and global citizens alike can aid in monk seal conservation efforts. We hope you will help us protect `Ilio holo i ka uaua, the Hawaiian name for the “dog that runs in rough water.”

Fun Facts

Hawaiian monk seals can hold their breath for up to 20 minutes and dive more than 1,800 feet; however, they usually dive an average of 6 minutes to depths of less than 200 feet to forage at the seafloor.

Monk seals live in warm, subtropical waters and spend two-thirds of their time at sea. They use the waters surrounding atolls and islands and areas farther offshore on reefs and submerged banks; they also use deepwater coral beds as foraging habitat.
